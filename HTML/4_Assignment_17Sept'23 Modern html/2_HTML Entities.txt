Q). What are HTML Entities? List five commonly used HTML entities.
Ans- HTML entities are special characters or Reserved characters that are used to represent non-ASCII characters in HTML code. 
     They are used to encode characters that have a different meaning or representation in HTML, such as ampersand(&),less than(<),
     and greater than(>). HTML entities are important for creating accurate and consistent HTML code, and for 
     ensuring that your content is displayed correctly in different browsers and platforms.
 
     Some commonly used HTML entities are-
     -> &lt; for less than sign
     -> &gt; for greater than sign
     -> &nbsp; for putting space in between
     -> &copy; for copyright symbol
     -> &amp; for putting ampersand sign
